📜 NEP 2020 – Foundational Stage Explained

National Education Policy 2020 | Section 1 of 5

What is NEP 2020?

The National Education Policy 2020 represents India's most comprehensive educational reform since 1986. It replaces the earlier 10+2 structure with a new 5+3+3+4 curricular framework that recognises the critical importance of early childhood learning and foundational literacy.

The 5+3+3+4 Framework:
  • Foundational Stage (5 yrs): Ages 3–8 | Pre-primary + Grades 1–2
  • Preparatory Stage (3 yrs): Grades 3–5 | Ages 8–11
  • Middle Stage (3 yrs): Grades 6–8 | Ages 11–14
  • Secondary Stage (4 yrs): Grades 9–12 | Ages 14–18

🎯 Foundational Literacy & Numeracy (FLN) Mission

NEP 2020 declares that achieving universal Foundational Literacy and Numeracy for all children by Grade 3 (by 2025) is the highest priority of the education system. The policy explicitly states that if a child cannot read, write, and perform basic arithmetic by Grade 3, all further schooling becomes compromised.

NIPUN Bharat Mission: Launched in 2021, the National Initiative for Proficiency in Reading with Understanding and Numeracy aims to ensure every child achieves grade-level competencies in reading and arithmetic by the end of Grade 3.

📌 Key Principles of NEP 2020 for Foundational Stage

  • Play-based learning: Activity and exploration-centred pedagogy for early years
  • Mother tongue instruction: Teaching in home/regional language until at least Grade 5
  • No bag, no homework: Joyful environment in early years
  • Holistic assessment: Progress based on competencies, not exams
  • Teacher capacity: Specialised training for foundational stage teachers
  • Integration: Seamless link between Anganwadi/preschool and primary school
  • Multilingualism: Three-language formula with emphasis on linguistic flexibility
  • Digital literacy: Age-appropriate introduction to digital tools from Grade 1

🏫 Classroom Implications for Grade 1–3 Teachers

NEP 2020 demands a fundamental shift in how foundational classrooms operate. The shift is from rote memorisation to conceptual understanding; from summative exams to formative, competency-based assessment; and from teacher-centric delivery to child-centric exploration.

What this means in practice: Teachers must shift from asking "What did the child memorise?" to "What can the child do independently?" This is the essence of competency-based education at the foundational level.

💬 Teacher Reflection Questions

  • How are you currently assessing reading progress — by marks or by demonstrated ability?
  • Are your Grade 1 students learning in their mother tongue before transitioning to Hindi/English?
  • What play-based activities do you use to teach phonics and letter recognition?
  • How do you track individual competency milestones without formal exams?
  • Do your classroom practices align with the joy-centred philosophy of NEP 2020?

📘 NCF 2023 – FLN Competency Structure

National Curriculum Framework for Foundational Stage

What is NCF 2023?

The National Curriculum Framework for the Foundational Stage (NCF-FS) 2022/2023 is the first ever standalone curriculum framework for children aged 3–8 in India. It operationalises the vision of NEP 2020 by providing specific competency targets, pedagogical approaches, and assessment strategies for the foundational stage.

Five Developmental Domains in NCF-FS:
  • 🧠 Cognitive Development (thinking, problem-solving)
  • 🗣️ Language & Literacy Development
  • 📐 Mathematical Thinking
  • 🌱 Social & Emotional Development
  • 💪 Physical Development & Motor Skills

📖 FLN Competency Structure in NCF 2023

NCF 2023 organises literacy competencies into clear, observable, and measurable learning outcomes. These are not tied to grade levels rigidly but to developmental progression.

Core Literacy Competencies:
  • Oral Language: Speaking clearly, listening with comprehension, vocabulary building
  • Phonological Awareness: Recognising sounds, rhyming, syllables
  • Print Awareness: Book handling, directionality, letter recognition
  • Decoding: Phonics, blending, word recognition
  • Reading Fluency: Speed, accuracy, expression
  • Reading Comprehension: Meaning-making, inference, critical thinking
  • Writing: Pre-writing, copying, independent writing

🎓 Pedagogical Approach in NCF 2023

NCF 2023 emphasises a structured literacy approach combined with meaning-focused instruction. It recommends explicit teaching of phonics alongside rich exposure to stories, poems, and meaningful texts in the child's mother tongue.

The Balanced Literacy Model:
  • Explicit phonics instruction (systematic, structured)
  • Shared reading with big books and stories
  • Guided reading in small groups
  • Independent reading time (free choice)
  • Read-alouds by teachers for modelling fluency

📊 Competency Progression Levels

NCF 2023 describes competency attainment across three stages: Emerging (the child is beginning to show the skill), Developing (the skill is inconsistent but improving), and Mastered (the child demonstrates the competency consistently and independently).

🔴 Emerging
Needs significant support. Skill not yet demonstrated independently.
🟡 Developing
Demonstrates skill with teacher support or inconsistently.
🟢 Mastered
Demonstrates skill independently, accurately, and consistently.

💬 Teacher Reflection Questions

  • How do you track each child's position on the competency progression ladder?
  • Are you providing both explicit phonics instruction AND meaning-rich reading experiences?
  • How do you differentiate instruction for children at different competency levels in the same class?
  • What mother tongue texts are you using in your classroom library?
  • How do you involve parents in supporting literacy at home?

📊 PARAKH – Diagnostic Assessment Principles

Performance Assessment, Review & Analysis of Knowledge for Holistic Development

What is PARAKH?

PARAKH (Performance Assessment, Review and Analysis of Knowledge for Holistic Development) is the National Assessment Centre established under NCERT as per NEP 2020. Its mandate is to standardise assessment norms across all school boards in India, shifting the focus from high-stakes examinations to holistic, formative, and competency-based assessment practices.

PARAKH's Core Mission: To ensure that assessments across India assess not just content knowledge but higher-order thinking skills, application, and values — creating a diagnostic rather than ranking function.

🔍 Types of Assessment in PARAKH Framework

Formative Assessment
Ongoing, during learning. Purpose: to guide teaching. Examples: observation, portfolios, peer-talk.
Summative Assessment
End of term. Purpose: to certify learning. Should be competency-based, not rote-recall.
Diagnostic Assessment
Before teaching. Purpose: to identify gaps and learning needs. Forms the basis for remediation planning.

📝 Assessment Principles for Foundational Stage

  • No written exams for Grades 1–2: Assessment must be through observation, activity, and oral interaction
  • Portfolio-based evidence: Collect samples of student work over time
  • Teacher observation records: Systematic anecdotal records of competency demonstration
  • Student self-assessment: Age-appropriate reflection using visual tools (smiley faces, RAG)
  • Peer assessment: Collaborative learning through buddy reading and peer feedback
  • Parent feedback integration: Home literacy reports included in holistic assessment

🚫 What Assessment Should NOT Be

  • ❌ Not about ranking children against each other
  • ❌ Not about identifying "weak" students publicly
  • ❌ Not about memorisation-only tests
  • ❌ Not about single-sitting high-stakes exams in early grades
  • ❌ Not about labelling children as failures
  • ❌ Not about marks or percentages in Grades 1–3

💬 Teacher Reflection Questions

  • Are your current assessments diagnostic (informing your teaching) or merely summative (recording results)?
  • How do you document individual child progress without formal written tests?
  • Do your assessments capture the full range of a child's abilities or only academic recall?
  • How do you use assessment data to plan remedial sessions?
  • Are parents informed about competency progress rather than just marks?

⚖️ Competency vs Marks-Based Learning

Understanding the paradigm shift in education

The Fundamental Shift

For decades, Indian education has been governed by a marks-based paradigm where success is measured by scores on standardised tests. NEP 2020, NCF 2023, and PARAKH collectively demand a shift to competency-based education (CBE), where success is measured by what a child can actually do.

📊 Comparison Table

Aspect Marks-Based Competency-Based
FocusContent recallSkill application
AssessmentWritten examsObservation, tasks, portfolios
ProgressA, B, C, D gradesEmerging/Developing/Mastered
FailurePossible, punitiveNo failure — only pace difference
Teacher roleExaminerFacilitator & observer
Child rolePassive receiverActive constructor of knowledge

🏆 Benefits of Competency-Based Education

  • Every child progresses at their own pace without being labelled as a "failure"
  • Teaching becomes targeted — teachers know exactly what each child needs next
  • Assessment is ongoing and formative, not just end-of-year
  • Children develop intrinsic motivation and confidence
  • It supports inclusion of children with diverse learning needs
  • It prepares children for real-life skills, not just test-taking strategies

💬 Teacher Reflection Questions

  • When you give a child 45/100, do you know exactly which competencies they are missing?
  • How do you track whether a child who scored 70% has actually mastered phonics?
  • What would your classroom look like if you assessed children daily through observation rather than weekly tests?
  • How would you explain competency-based progress to parents who ask "how many marks did my child get?"
  • Can a child with 80% marks still be an "emerging" reader? How would you know?

📋 Holistic Progress Card Framework

Moving beyond marks to meaningful reporting

What is a Holistic Progress Card (HPC)?

A Holistic Progress Card replaces the traditional marks-based report card with a competency-based record that captures the whole child — cognitive abilities, social-emotional development, physical development, creative expression, and values. It is designed to communicate growth to parents in a meaningful, actionable way.

HPC vs Traditional Report Card:
  • HPC uses descriptors (Emerging/Developing/Mastered) instead of percentages
  • HPC includes all five developmental domains, not just academics
  • HPC is generated 2–3 times per year, not just at year-end
  • HPC includes teacher observations, not just test scores
  • HPC has space for child self-assessment and parent comments

🗂️ Components of an Effective HPC

📖 Language & Literacy
Letter recognition, phonics, reading fluency, comprehension, writing
🔢 Mathematical Thinking
Number sense, operations, shapes, patterns
🌍 Socio-Emotional
Self-regulation, empathy, teamwork, confidence
🏃 Physical Development
Gross motor, fine motor, health habits

🔢 Rating Scale Options

Different schools use different systems. PARAKH recommends:

  • 🔴 Not Yet (NY): Competency not yet demonstrated
  • 🟡 Developing (D): With support or inconsistently demonstrated
  • 🟢 Achieved (A): Consistently and independently demonstrated

💬 Teacher Reflection Questions

  • Does your current progress report capture the child's growth as a whole person or only as an academic performer?
  • How do you explain to parents what "Developing" means for their child's reading competency?
  • What evidence do you collect to fill in a holistic progress card honestly?
  • How often do you share progress information with families — termly, monthly, or on request only?
  • How can you make HPCs accessible to parents with low literacy levels?

📱 PWA & APK Conversion Guide

Step 1: Export Static Build
  • The entire app is already a static HTML file — no build needed!
  • Folder: fln-reading-app/ containing index.html, manifest.json, service-worker.js, author.jpg
Step 2: Host as PWA
  • Upload to GitHub Pages: Create repo → push files → enable Pages in Settings
  • Upload to Netlify: Drag folder to netlify.com/drop
  • Upload to Cloudflare Pages: Free hosting with global CDN
  • Use VS Code Live Server for local testing
Step 3: Convert to APK (TWA Method)
  • 1. Host your PWA at a public HTTPS URL
  • 2. Install Android Studio + Bubblewrap CLI: npm install -g @bubblewrap/cli
  • 3. Run: bubblewrap init --manifest https://yoursite.com/manifest.json
  • 4. Build APK: bubblewrap build
  • 5. Sign with keystore and distribute
Step 3 Alternative: WebView APK
  • 1. Create Android Studio project
  • 2. Add WebView to MainActivity: webView.loadUrl("file:///android_asset/index.html")
  • 3. Copy HTML files to app/src/main/assets/
  • 4. Enable JavaScript: webView.settings.javaScriptEnabled = true
  • 5. Build → Generate Signed APK → Distribute via WhatsApp or email
