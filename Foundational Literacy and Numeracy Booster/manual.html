CHAPTER 01

Getting Started & Installation

The FLN Booster is a Progressive Web App (PWA). This means it works in any modern web browser — no app store required — and can be installed on your device for offline use.

1

Open the App

Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Edge recommended) and navigate to the app URL. The app loads immediately.

2

Install to Home Screen (Android)

On Android Chrome, tap the three-dot menu (⋮) at the top-right, then tap "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App". Confirm installation. The app now appears on your home screen like a native app.

3

Install on Desktop (Windows/Mac)

In Chrome, click the install icon (⊕) in the address bar, or go to Settings → "Install FLN Booster". Once installed, the app runs in its own window without the browser toolbar.

4

Use Offline

After first load, the app works completely offline. All lessons, assessments, and progress are stored on your device. No internet connection needed after initial setup.

💡 Teacher Tip: To share the app without internet in your school, run it on a local server (e.g., VS Code Live Server) and connect all student devices to the same WiFi network. Students can then install it and use it offline permanently.

Running locally: Download all 3 files (index.html, manifest.json, service-worker.js) into a folder. Right-click on the folder → "Open with Live Server" (VS Code extension). The app opens at localhost:5500.

CHAPTER 03

Diagnostic Assessment

The diagnostic assessment is the recommended starting point. It identifies whether a student's gaps are in literacy, numeracy, or both — enabling the app to guide them to the right modules first. This is aligned with PARAKH's competency-based assessment philosophy.

1

Starting the Test

From the Dashboard, tap "🔍 Quick Diagnostic Assessment". The test begins immediately with 10 questions spanning literacy and numeracy.

2

Answering Questions

Each question shows 4 answer options. Tap your choice. The correct answer immediately highlights in green; wrong answers show in red. The test auto-advances to the next question after 1.2 seconds.

3

Progress Bar

The orange progress bar at the top of the assessment screen shows how many questions you have completed out of 10.

4

Results Screen

After 10 questions, your score is displayed. Based on your score: 0-3 → Beginner (focus on foundational modules), 4-6 → Developing (work through all modules), 7-10 → Proficient (use harder difficulty levels).

5

Assessment History

Every completed diagnostic is saved and visible in the Progress tab. This allows teachers to track improvement over retakes.

📘 NCF/PARAKH Note: This diagnostic is a formative, low-stakes assessment tool — not a graded exam. Encourage students to attempt it honestly and retake it after practice to see growth.

CHAPTER 04

Reading Development Module

The reading module has three integrated components: Phonics Practice, Story Reading, and Comprehension Questions. Together these develop fluency and understanding — core NCF 2022 reading competencies.

1

Choose Your Level

Use the difficulty selector at the top: 🟢 Beginner (simple phonics and short texts), 🟡 Developing (medium complexity), 🔴 Proficient (advanced comprehension). Start at Beginner and progress upward.

2

Phonics Practice Grid

The grid shows 16 letter tiles (A–P). Tap any letter to see its phonetic sound and a key example word (e.g., "A says /æ/ — like Apple"). This builds letter-sound correspondence awareness. Each tap adds to your reading progress score.

3

Story Reading

A short, age-appropriate story is displayed with clickable words. Tap any word to highlight it in yellow. This encourages word-by-word reading focus. Each word interaction adds to your progress. Tap "Next Story →" to load a new story.

4

Comprehension Questions

After the story, 3 comprehension questions test understanding (not memory). Each has 4 options. Select your answer — correct answers earn progress points and move you toward the Reading Star badge.

💡 Classroom Use: Have students read the story aloud before answering comprehension questions. For very weak readers, the teacher reads the story aloud while students follow along on screen — this is a shared reading strategy aligned with NCF literacy pedagogy.

CHAPTER 05

Mathematics Module

The math module presents word problems rooted in everyday Indian contexts — emphasizing the NEP 2020 principle that mathematics should be connected to real life. Three difficulty levels cover the progression from simple counting to multiplication.

1

Select Difficulty Level

🟢 Counting: Simple addition and subtraction within 10. 🟡 Add/Sub: Two-digit operations with real-world contexts. 🔴 Multiply: Multiplication problems with practical scenarios. Begin at the level matching your diagnostic result.

2

Word Problem

A word problem appears in the grey box above the equation (e.g., "Raju has 4 apples. His mother gives him 3 more. How many does he have?"). Read the problem carefully before solving.

3

Number Equation

Below the word problem, the equation is shown in large numerals (e.g., "4 + 3 = ?"). The answer box below awaits your input.

4

Number Pad

Use the on-screen number pad to enter your answer without a keyboard. Tap digits to build your number, use ⌫ to delete, and ✓ (green) to submit. This is optimised for touch devices.

5

Feedback & Next Problem

If correct, a green "✅ Correct! Well done!" message appears and your score updates. If wrong, the correct answer is shown. The next problem loads automatically after 1.5 seconds. Your session score is shown as X/Total.

💡 Teacher Tip for Simulations: For very basic learners, act out the word problem physically. "Raju has 4 apples — put 4 objects on the desk. Now add 3 more. Count all together." Then enter the answer in the app. This bridges concrete-pictorial-abstract learning, as recommended by NCF 2022.

CHAPTER 06

Writing Skills Module

The writing module provides structured, scaffolded writing prompts across three levels — from basic letter formation to full paragraph writing. This is aligned with NCF 2022's emphasis on developing written expression as a core foundational competency.

1

Select Writing Level

🟢 Letters: Practice copying letters, words, and short sentences. 🟡 Sentences: Write 4-5 sentences on guided topics. 🔴 Paragraph: Compose full paragraphs on academic and real-world themes.

2

The Writing Prompt

The dark blue prompt card at the top shows your writing task title and detailed instructions. Read the prompt carefully before beginning. For Paragraph level, specific ideas and vocabulary cues are provided.

3

The Writing Area (Lined Paper)

The writing area uses a lined background mimicking actual ruled paper — helping students develop consistent line use. Type your response here. The word counter updates in real time at the bottom right.

4

Save Your Writing

Tap "💾 Save Writing" to save your entry to local storage. Up to 10 recent entries are saved and displayed below the writing area for review. Saving earns progress points toward the Writing Champion badge.

5

Alphabet Reference Grid

A full A-Z alphabet reference grid at the bottom of the Writing module helps beginners who need to reference letter shapes while writing.

📝 Pedagogical Note: For Letter-level students, the teacher should model each letter on the board before the student types it in the app. For Sentence-level, use the "talk first, write later" strategy — have students verbally describe their response before writing. For Paragraph level, teach the basic paragraph structure (topic sentence → supporting sentences → conclusion) before the student writes.

CHAPTER 07

Progress Tracking & Badges

The Progress tab provides a holistic view of the learner's journey, aligned with PARAKH's continuous progress monitoring philosophy.

1

Module Progress Bars

Three progress bars show completion percentage for Reading, Math, and Writing. Progress increases as students complete activities in each module. Aim for 100% in each module.

2

Achievement Badges

Three badges can be earned: ⭐ Reading Star (complete 40% reading), 🔭 Math Explorer (complete 40% math), 🏆 Writing Champion (save 2+ writing pieces). Earned badges glow gold with a celebration toast notification.

3

Assessment History

All completed diagnostic assessments are listed with date and score. This allows students and teachers to track progress over time and identify if retaken scores improve.

4

Reset Progress

The red "Reset All Progress" button clears all data — useful when a new student starts using the same device. All saved writings and assessment history are permanently deleted.

CHAPTER 08

For Teachers — Classroom Use

Teachers can integrate this app into NEP 2020-aligned classroom practice in several structured ways.

1

Identification Phase

Begin each academic year by having all students complete the Diagnostic Assessment. Students scoring below 5/10 are identified as requiring FLN support — these students use the app in Beginner mode first.

2

Remediation Groups

Group students by diagnostic score. While advanced students do grade-level work, the FLN group uses the app for 20-30 minutes daily. Rotate device access if limited devices are available.

3

Shared Reading Strategy

For the Reading module: display the story on a classroom screen/projector and read aloud as a class. Ask comprehension questions verbally before students answer individually on the app. This is a classic shared reading approach aligned with NCF 2022.

4

Math Simulation with Objects

For Math module word problems: use physical objects (stones, erasers, pencils) to simulate the problem before entering the digital answer. This bridges the concrete-to-abstract gap that many struggling learners need.

5

Progress Review

At end of month, review Progress tab with each FLN student individually. Celebrate badge achievements. Record module progress percentages in your class register as formative assessment data.

CHAPTER 09

For Parents — Home Use

1

Daily Practice Routine

Set aside 20 minutes each evening for app practice. Suggested routine: 7 minutes Reading (2-3 phonics tiles + story), 7 minutes Math (5-6 problems), 6 minutes Writing (one writing prompt, saved).

2

Reviewing Progress Together

Once a week, open the Progress tab with your child and review the progress bars together. Celebrate new badges. Ask your child to explain one story they read or one math problem they solved.

3

No Internet Required

Once installed, the app works fully offline. It can be used during power cuts (on a charged device), during travel, or anywhere — making home learning truly accessible in all conditions.

CHAPTER 10

Hosting & Android Distribution

To share this app with your school, district, or community:

1

GitHub Pages (Free)

Create a free GitHub account → New repository → Upload all 4 files → Go to Settings → Pages → Source: main branch. Your app will be live at: username.github.io/fln-booster

2

Netlify (Free, Easiest)

Go to netlify.com → Drag your project folder into the browser → Netlify instantly deploys and gives you a public URL. Automatic HTTPS included.

3

Convert to Android APK (PWABuilder)

Visit pwabuilder.com → Enter your hosted URL → Click "Build" → Select Android → Download the APK. Install the APK on any Android device, or upload to Google Play Store.

4

Google Play Store

Create a Google Play Developer account ($25 one-time fee) → Upload the APK/AAB from PWABuilder → Fill in store listing (name, description, screenshots) → Submit for review (usually 1-3 days) → Publish.

💡 For Schools Without Internet: Use a USB drive to transfer the project folder to each device. Open with VS Code Live Server or any local server tool. Students on the same WiFi can access the app for installation.
